Cheesecake Recipe

Recipe source: Joy Food Sunshine

Image source: Joy Food Sunshine

Ingredients:

    For the crust:
  • 1 and 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs (12 full cracker sheets)
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 6 tablespoons salted butter, melted

    • For the filling:
  • 24 oz cream cheese, room temperature (three 8 oz packages)
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon sea salt
  • 1/2 cup sour cream, room temperature
  • 3 large eggs, room temperature
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour

Instructions:

  1. Butter and flour the sides of a 9” spring form pan. Line the bottom of the pan completely with heavy-duty foil so no water can leak into the pan from the water bath. Set aside.
  2. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F.
  3. Make the graham cracker crust:In a large bowl, combine graham cracker crumbs, sugar and cinnamon. Add melted butter and stir until combined.
  4. Pour the crust mixture into the prepared springform pan, pressing it down into the bottom of the pan and halfway up the sides.
  5. Bake in preheated oven for 5 minutes, then remove the crust from the oven and set aside to slightly cool.
  6. Make the cheesecake filling: While the crust is baking make the filling. In a standing mixer fitted with the paddle attachment or in a large bowl with a handheld mixer, beat together the cream cheese and sugar until light and fluffy and there are no lumps.
  7. Add vanilla, sea salt and sour cream and beat until just combined.
  8. Add eggs, one at a time and beat after each addition.
  9. Gently stir in flour.
  10. Prepare a water bath: Use a large pan that will fit your cake pan and put it in the preheated oven. Bring 2 quarts of water to a boil.
  11. Bake: Pour the filling over the baked and slightly cooled crust.
  12. Place the cake pan in a large pan in the preheated oven. Slowly fill the large pan with boiling water until it is halfway up the sides of the cheesecake pan.
  13. Bake in the water bath for 45-55 minutes or until the top and edges are set but not browned and the cake is only very slightly jiggly. You can test it by inserting a knife in the center of the cake and if it comes out clean you know the cake is done, however this can cause the cheesecake to crack so I don’t really recommend it (but it’s better than a soupy cheesecake if you’re unsure).
  14. Remove the cheesecake from the water bath and set on a wire cooling rack to cool to room temperature. Once at room temperature, transfer the cheesecake to the refrigerator to chill overnight.
  15. Top with whipped cream.
